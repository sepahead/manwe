## 1. Vision detection for small aerial objects / anti-UAV / drone-vs-bird

### SOTA overview

As of mid-2026 the accuracy–latency Pareto front for real-time detection is led by **DETR-family transformers**, not classic YOLO. [RF-DETR](https://github.com/roboflow/rf-detr) (Roboflow, ICLR 2026, DINOv2 backbone + weight-sharing NAS) is the first real-time detector to pass 60 AP on COCO and dominates the Roboflow100-VL domain-adaptation benchmark, making it the strongest fine-tuning base. [RT-DETRv4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25257) and [DEIM](https://github.com/Intellindust-AI-Lab/DEIM)-trained [D-FINE](https://github.com/Peterande/D-FINE) lead the pure-COCO transformer race in the Apache-licensed lane, while [Ultralytics YOLO26](https://docs.ultralytics.com/models/yolo26/) (NMS-free end-to-end) and [YOLOv11/YOLOv12](https://github.com/sunsmarterjie/yolov12) remain the best-tooled CNN track with by far the most mature CoreML/ANE export on Apple Silicon.

For **small aerial objects specifically, the input pipeline and detector architecture are complementary levers**: [SAHI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.06934) slicing, a stride-4 [P2 high-res head](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/26/7/2030), and [TPH-YOLO](https://github.com/cv516Buaa/tph-yolov5) transformer prediction heads have each reported gains on small-object aerial benchmarks, but the cited studies do not establish a universal ranking among them. The canonical training/eval sets are [Anti-UAV410](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10325629/) (thermal IR), [Drone-vs-Bird/WOSDETC](https://wosdetc2025.wordpress.com/) (RGB with bird distractors, essential for false-positive suppression), [VisDrone](https://arxiv.org/html/2504.11967v2) (small-object aerial), and air-to-air sets like [ARD-MAV](https://arxiv.org/html/2503.07115v1).

### Recommended picks

- **Primary (accuracy/fine-tuning track):** RF-DETR — Apache-permissive sizes, DINOv2 generalization, strongest domain adaptation.
- **Primary (latency/tooling track):** YOLO26 or YOLO11 — cleanest CoreML/ANE + MPS + CUDA path.
- **CUDA-side accuracy references:** D-FINE, RT-DETRv4 (both Apache).
- **Input-pipeline and architecture ablation:** SAHI (train + eval) + P2 head, optionally QueryDet-style sparse queries for compute budget.
- **Auto-labeling:** Grounding DINO 1.5 Pro (quality) + YOLO-World/YOLOE (fast) → distill into the closed-set detector.

### Caveats & Apple-Silicon gaps

- **⚠️ Caution — "RF-DETR is Apache-2.0" is misleading.** Verification found only the **code and Nano→Large models are Apache-2.0; RF-DETR-XL and RF-DETR-2XL (the 60.1-AP flagship) ship under PML 1.0**, *not* Apache. This is load-bearing for a shippable product — do not treat the 60-AP model as license-clean. ([source](https://github.com/roboflow/rf-detr))
- **⚠️ Caution — SAHI as "the biggest lever, not architecture."** The empirical gains (+5.1–6.8% AP sliced inference, +12.7–14.5% cumulative with slicing-aided fine-tuning on VisDrone) are **verified and correctly attributed**, but the *"tiling beats architecture"* framing is an **editorial overreach the SAHI paper does not make** — the paper positions slicing as complementary to architecture. Treat both as orthogonal, stacking levers.
- Transformer detectors (RF-DETR, D-FINE, RT-DETR) often **fall back to GPU on the ANE** because deformable/multi-scale attention maps poorly to the Neural Engine — verify op placement per export. CNN YOLO exports cleanly and runs on ANE.
- **Licensing fork:** RF-DETR (core), D-FINE, DEIM, RT-DETRv2/v4 are Apache-2.0; **Ultralytics YOLO (v8/11/12/26) is AGPL-3.0** — a research/benchmark track unless commercially licensed.
- MLX detection training remains experimental. Use PyTorch/MPS (Apple) and CUDA (NVIDIA) as the established training paths; evaluate native MLX training only with pinned artifacts and independently reproduced accuracy and performance.
- No authoritative public **"Halcyon"** counter-UAS dataset could be located; the closest documented multi-sensor set is the **Halmstad** RGB+IR+audio(+radar) collection.

## 2. Acoustic drone detection, classification & DOA with audio-visual fusion

### SOTA overview

Acoustic counter-UAS has bifurcated into two lines manwe should fuse. **(1) Sound Event Localization and Detection (SELD)**, driven by the DCASE challenges on [STARSS23](https://dcase.community/challenge2024/task-audio-and-audiovisual-sound-event-localization-and-detection-with-source-distance-estimation-results), is the right formulation for producing **azimuth/elevation/range**: it jointly does event detection, DOA regression, and (since DCASE 2024) source-distance estimation. The dominant architecture is a **ResNet-Conformer CRNN** on multichannel log-mel + [SALSA-Lite](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.08192) spatial features. Linear-complexity [SELD-Mamba](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05057) is the emerging trend.

**(2) Drone-specific audio-visual trajectory work** centers on [MMAUD](https://github.com/ntu-aris/MMAUD) (mic array + LiDAR + radar + vision, mm-accurate Leica ground truth). Self-supervised audio-visual Mamba ([AV-DTEC](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.16928): 99.3% class, 0.67 m error, 10 M params) beats the supervised [AV-FDTI](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2949855424000285) baseline; audio-only [TAME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.13037) reaches 98% class / 0.55 m. Classification backbones have moved from PANNs/AST to self-supervised BEATs/EAT and the current polyphonic SOTA [SSLAM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.12222) (0.502 mAP, ICLR 2025).

### Recommended picks

- **Core acoustic model candidate:** a SELD head with **Multi-ACCDOA** output can
  represent per-class activity, Cartesian DOA and distance. A crebain adapter must
  still add the exact frame, angle, covariance, time, ID and range-observability
  semantics; matching fields is not native contract compatibility.
- **Default backbone:** ResNet-Conformer (pure conv+attention, trains on MPS+CUDA). Treat SELD-Mamba/AV-DTEC/TAME as a **CUDA-only** high-accuracy track.
- **Features:** log-mel + SALSA-Lite (planar array) or FOA intensity (ambisonic); add GCC-PHAT/SRP-PHAT as auxiliary channels + training-free fallback.
- **Classifier:** init from SSLAM/EAT/BEATs (AudioSet), fine-tune on drone corpora; ship PANNs CNN14/AST to CoreML/ANE for edge.
- **AV fusion:** reproduce AV-FDTI first (MPS/CUDA-portable), then AV-DTEC-style residual cross-attention.
- **SPL:** compute directly (calibrated per-mic dB SPL), do not learn it; feed SPL + DOA + range as observations to the fusion tracker.

### Caveats & Apple-Silicon gaps

- **⚠️ Caution — DCASE'24 SELD numbers came back *partially-supported*.** The general figures (audio-only ≈54.4% F / 13.6° DOA, AV fusion improving DOA to ≈11.4°) reflect the winning line, but treat exact per-system numbers as approximate; the "solved formulation for az/el/range" framing is a reasonable engineering read, not a settled benchmark fact. ([DCASE](https://dcase.community/challenge2024/task-audio-and-audiovisual-sound-event-localization-and-detection-with-source-distance-estimation-results))
- **Mamba selective-scan needs CUDA kernels** (selective-scan + causal-conv1d) with **no first-class Metal implementation** — heavy Mamba training is NVIDIA-only.
- **⚠️ Caution — "polyphonic pretraining matches airspace audio"** is an unproven domain-transfer analogy; SSLAM's polyphonic benchmarks are urban/speech scenes, never drone/airspace audio. The +9.1% mAP figure itself is verified.
- AV-DTEC/TAME/AV-FDTI results are **single-lab (NTU ARIS), single-dataset (MMAUD), no third-party reproduction**, and MMAUD V1 is the *easier* subset (drones <30 m); harder V2/V3 reach ~100 m. "Self-supervised" here means LiDAR pseudo-labels, not classic SSL.
- Acoustic range is short (~50–60 m per MEMS node) and deep methods collapse below ~−30 dB SNR — plan a **distributed** acoustic layer with weak-range, good-bearing covariances.
- MLX has no mature mic-array/audio-SSL training stack — MLX is inference/export-only for audio.

## 3. Multi-camera detection, 3D tracking, calibration & triangulation

### SOTA overview

Multi-camera 3D perception splits into two families. **(1) Learned BEV early-fusion** ([MVDet](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2007.07247) → [MVDeTr](https://github.com/hou-yz/MVDeTr) → [EarlyBird](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.13350) → [TrackTacular](https://github.com/tteepe/TrackTacular) → [BEV-SUSHI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.00692)) projects per-camera features onto a shared ground-plane BEV grid and detects/tracks there; SOTA on WILDTRACK/AI-City (BEV-SUSHI HOTA 81.22 on AICity'24). **Critically, these assume targets stand on a known ground plane and are trained on pedestrians/vehicles — they do NOT natively represent airborne drones at altitude.**

**(2) Classical geometric triangulation** — undistort, back-project 2D detections to rays, solve N-view [DLT](https://amytabb.com/tips/tutorials/2021/10/31/triangulation-DLT-2-3/) (SVD null-space) or [midpoint least-squares](https://openmvg.readthedocs.io/en/latest/openMVG/multiview/multiview/) for a 3D point with covariance — is a cross-platform candidate backbone. It still depends on calibrated intrinsics/distortion/extrinsics, synchronization, correspondence, observability and a declared world frame; its output does not feed a consumer tracker directly without that adapter. Calibration candidates: [Zhang/OpenCV](https://docs.opencv.org/4.x/dc/dbb/tutorial_py_calibration.html) checkerboard (intrinsics), [COLMAP](https://github.com/colmap/colmap) bundle adjustment or PnP (extrinsics), [GeoCalib](https://github.com/cvg/GeoCalib)/[AnyCalib](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.12701) as single-image fallbacks.

### Recommended picks

- **Backbone:** classical N-view triangulation (Rust `nalgebra` / numpy): DLT via SVD **and** midpoint ray LSQ, confidence-weighted rows, RANSAC over camera subsets, reprojection gating, per-point covariance (LOSTU-style). Cross-platform, no custom ops.
- **Correspondence before triangulation:** epipolar/Sampson gating from the fundamental matrix, confirmed with ReID (OSNet/TransReID — AI-City winning recipe).
- **Calibration tiers:** Zhang (owned cams) → COLMAP/PnP (extrinsics) → GeoCalib/AnyCalib (single-image fallback).
- **Tracker integration candidate:** adapt triangulated position/covariance through
  an explicit frame/time/ID schema and validate it against crebain fixtures.
- **Learned BEV = optional ground-target path only**, scoped to people/vehicles near a protected site; start with TrackTacular (most MPS-portable), treat BEV-SUSHI/BEVFormer as CUDA-only.

### Caveats & Apple-Silicon gaps

- **⚠️ Caution — "all BEV methods use a flat ground-plane occupancy grid" is *partially-supported*.** The load-bearing conclusion (these methods are ground-anchored, trained only on ground-standing targets, do not output a free altitude axis) **holds**, but TrackTacular internally lifts to a 3D voxel grid and BEV-SUSHI outputs full 3D boxes — the representation *can* carry a vertical axis; the limitation is the ground-rest assumption and training data. "Full-3D triangulation is *required*" overstates exclusivity (extending the BEV lift to a voxel volume is an equally valid path). ([TrackTacular](https://arxiv.org/html/2403.12573v1), [BEV-SUSHI](https://arxiv.org/html/2412.00692v1))
- **BEVFormer/deformable-attention custom ops do not run on Apple MPS** — the CUDA-only gap is real; verify TrackTacular actually trains/infers on MPS before committing.
- **No public airborne multi-camera triangulation benchmark exists** — generate a synthetic aerial set (Omniverse/Blender/AirSim) with known 3D drone trajectories + exact calibration.
- **Time synchronization (hardware trigger/PTP) is a hard prerequisite** — triangulation is invalid across unsynchronized frames.

## 4. Multi-target tracking & multi-sensor fusion (+ simulation/eval harness)

### SOTA overview

The field splits into two mature-but-separate stacks. **(1) Model-based Bayesian tracking/fusion** — KF/EKF/UKF/PF, IMM for maneuvering targets, GNN/JPDA/MHT association, and random-finite-set filters that have moved from PHD/CPHD to closed-form labeled/PMBM families ([GLMB/LMB](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.02772), [PMBM/TPMBM](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12714792/), with 2024-26 jump-Markov MM-TPMBM variants). The reference open-source harness is [Stone Soup](https://github.com/dstl/Stone-Soup) (MIT, pure Python), cross-checked against [García-Fernández's MTT](https://github.com/Agarciafernandez/MTT) library. **(2) Learned sensor-fusion detectors** — camera-radar ([CRN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.00670), [CenterFusion](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2011.04841)) and lidar-camera ([BEVFusion](https://github.com/mit-han-lab/bevfusion), [TransFusion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.11496)) BEV fusion — plus transformer trackers ([MOTR/MOTRv2](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2211.09791)) that lead on non-linear DanceTrack while tracking-by-detection ([ByteTrack, OC-SORT](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2302.11813)) stays faster on pedestrian benchmarks.

For **evaluation**, [HOTA](https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.07736) (via [TrackEval](https://github.com/JonathonLuiten/TrackEval)) is the primary MOT metric but is **not** a true mathematical metric; [GOSPA/T-GOSPA](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.13706v1) are the mathematically consistent set metrics with clean localization/missed/false/switch decompositions — the right primary metric for a Bayesian fusion tracker.

### Recommended picks

- **Offline harness:** Stone Soup — build a cross-check tracker from
  ConstantVelocity 6-state + DistanceHypothesiser(Mahalanobis) chi-square gating
  + GNN (→ JPDA) + MultiMeasurementInitiator (M-of-N) + deleter. Use it as an
  independent reference only where algorithms and conventions overlap.
- **Primary metrics:** GOSPA + T-GOSPA (from García-Fernández's Python T-GOSPA spec) for the fusion filter; TrackEval HOTA/IDF1 for the video/multi-cam side. Wire both into CI.
- **Proposed production-validation path:** a Rust tracker cross-checked against an
  independently specified reference on shared intermediate-state fixtures. Stone
  Soup is one candidate reference, not proof of parity by itself.
- **Near-term upgrade:** implement IMM (CV + constant-turn + constant-accel) in an
  independently specified Python reference, then port it to Rust. Stone Soup can
  cross-check the overlapping single-model components, but not IMM itself.
  Longer-term: GM-PHD → GLMB/PMBM for dense swarms.
- **Learned front-end:** CenterFusion → CRN-style radar-camera fusion feeding the Bayesian tracker.

### Caveats & Apple-Silicon gaps

- **⚠️ Caution — Stone Soup does NOT implement an IMM predictor or GLMB filter.** Verification against a fresh v1.9.1 checkout found **no IMM/Interacting-Multiple-Model class and no GLMB filter** — these were fabricated in the source claim. Stone Soup *does* have a multiple-model *particle* filter (a distinct jump-Markov algorithm) and GM-PHD/LCC-PHD/Bernoulli filters, plus MFA (not classical MHT) association. **Plan to hand-roll IMM and any labeled-RFS filter yourself**, cross-validating against García-Fernández's MATLAB MTT. The rest of the Stone Soup claim (v1.9.1, 2026-06-24, MIT, pure Python, KF/EKF/UKF/PF, GNN/PDA/JPDA, GM-PHD, Mahalanobis gating, M-of-N init, OSPA/GOSPA/SIAP, simulators, no GPU accel) is **verified**. ([Stone Soup](https://github.com/dstl/Stone-Soup))
- **⚠️ Caution — CRN "ranks first among camera/camera-radar detectors" is stale.** True at ICCV'23 publication, but **superseded by RCBEVDet (CVPR'24), HyDRa, CRT-Fusion, RaCFormer (CVPR'25)** by mid-2026. The 62.4 NDS / 57.5 mAP / 20 FPS figures are verified; the "ranks first" is a point-in-time claim. Also, both CRN and BEVFusion are **nuScenes automotive** benchmarks — the "most relevant regime for counter-UAS" is engineering inference, not established by those sources. ([CRN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.00670))
- **No mature Rust library implements JPDA, IMM, or RFS filters** — Rust crates are SORT/ByteTrack/OC-SORT-class only. The model-based core stays hand-rolled Rust + Python/MATLAB reference.
- Stone Soup and García-Fernández MTT are **research-grade, offline, no GPU** — not real-time.
- Learned radar/lidar fusion nets use **custom CUDA BEV-pooling/sparse ops** needing real re-implementation for MPS/MLX.

## 5. Training/fine-tuning recipes & model export toolchains

### SOTA overview

A candidate path from data to backend-specific artifacts is a config-driven [Ultralytics YOLO26](https://docs.ultralytics.com/models/yolo26/) pipeline exported via the [one-call export API](https://docs.ultralytics.com/modes/export/). It does not by itself satisfy crebain's four distinct runtime contracts. For a higher accuracy candidate, [RF-DETR](https://github.com/roboflow/rf-detr) is designed for fine-tuning, but its DETR outputs require a different adapter. Candidate recipe components include Mosaic + MixUp + [Copy-Paste](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12524), [AlbumentationsX](https://github.com/albumentations-team/AlbumentationsX), [SAHI](https://github.com/obss/sahi), EMA, LR scheduling, AMP and optional distillation; each must be ablated on the target data.

**Export is where fidelity is lost.** PyTorch→ONNX (opset/simplify/dynamic, NMS external) → [TensorRT](https://docs.ultralytics.com/integrations/tensorrt/) now uses **NVIDIA ModelOpt explicit Q/DQ quantization** (TensorRT 11 removed the old IInt8Calibrator) needing ~500+ calibration images → [coremltools](https://apple.github.io/coremltools/docs-guides/source/opt-overview.html) compiled to `.mlmodelc` with iOS18 palettization/INT4 for ANE → MLX via the native [yolo-mlx](https://github.com/thewebAI/yolo-mlx) port. [Microsoft Olive](https://github.com/microsoft/Olive) automates ONNX quantization sweeps.

### Recommended picks

- **One trained `.pt` = single source of truth.** Config-driven (YAML/Hydra) YOLO26 trainer + parallel RF-DETR track.
- **Augmentation:** Mosaic + MixUp + Copy-Paste with close_mosaic final epochs + AlbumentationsX + SAHI (train + inference).
- **Recipe primitives (log them):** EMA, cosine/one-cycle LR, AMP, MuSGD; optional FGD distillation into n/s students.
- **Proposed export fan-out** → pinned ONNX, deployment-specific TensorRT,
  compiled CoreML, and a separately implemented MLX conversion. Manwe currently
  implements only the raw ONNX/CoreML/TensorRT conversion portion.
- **Fidelity work:** Manwe's current gate reports threshold-specific AP50 and a
  simplified AP50-small delta, and also gates deployed-threshold
  precision/recall/FPPI plus direct per-frame/class box and score agreement. Full
  COCO `mAP@[.50:.95]`, calibration and consumer end-to-end fixtures remain
  required.

### Caveats & Apple-Silicon gaps

- **⚠️ Caution — YOLO26 "INT8 retains near-FP32 accuracy" is unsupported by the cited sources.** The *design intent* of quantization-friendliness (DFL removed, NMS-free) is documented, but the *outcome* claim is not in the YOLO26 docs or paper — the TensorRT integration page actually presents INT8 as an **accuracy trade-off**. Treat INT8 accuracy as something the export-fidelity harness must *measure*, not assume. (Everything else about YOLO26 is verified.) ([docs](https://docs.ultralytics.com/models/yolo26/))
- **⚠️ Caution — yolo-mlx speed/parity numbers are vendor self-reported and internally inconsistent.** Every figure is webAI's own benchmark, **not independently reproduced**; the launch blog (1.1–2.6× inference, ~1.7× training, <0.5% mAP) is already **contradicted by the project's own later README** (up to 2.07× inference, 2.65× training). The sub-0.5% parity holds only against webAI's self-run baseline, which is ~0.6–0.8 mAP below Ultralytics' *published* numbers — against the real official model the gap is up to ~1.1%. **Re-benchmark on target hardware before trusting.** ([repo](https://github.com/thewebAI/yolo-mlx))
- **⚠️ Caution — RF-DETR-L "beats YOLOv11x (54.7 AP)" mixes measurement protocols.** RF-DETR's own tables report YOLOv11x at **50.9 AP** (its protocol); 54.7 is the Ultralytics-official number under a different protocol. The direction (RF-DETR-L wins at lower latency) holds under either, but the specific 54.7 comparison is apples-to-oranges. Also re-confirm the Apache/PML license split (§1). ([RF-DETR](https://github.com/roboflow/rf-detr))
- **⚠️ Caution — coremltools "9.0 adds 4-bit quantization / 3-bit palettization / stateful models" is misattributed.** Verification against the GitHub API shows these shipped in **coremltools 8.0 (Sept 2024)**, a year earlier. What 9.0 (2025-11-10) genuinely adds: **PyTorch 2.7 + ExecuTorch 0.5, Python 3.13, int8 model I/O, iOS26/macOS26 targets.** Still upgrade to 9.0 — just credit the compression features correctly. ([releases](https://github.com/apple/coremltools/releases))
- **TensorRT is NVIDIA-only** (no Apple path). CoreML needs FLOAT32 for iOS16 NMS layers and FP16/palettization can silently drift. MLX training is the newest, least battle-tested link and conversion tooling may still need PyTorch. **AGPL license** on AlbumentationsX for a commercial build.

## 6. Hardware acceleration: Apple Metal AND NVIDIA CUDA

### SOTA overview

**NVIDIA CUDA is the mature default for the proposed large-training workflow** because it offers established [torch.compile/Triton](https://tunguz.github.io/PyTorch_Hardware_2025/), [FlashAttention-3](https://github.com/Dao-AILab/flash-attention), low-precision and NCCL-based [DDP/FSDP2](https://pytorch.org/blog/maximizing-training/) paths. That is a workload-dependent engineering choice, not a universal hardware ranking. [PyTorch's MPS backend](https://developer.apple.com/metal/pytorch/) has different dtype, compiler, distributed and operator coverage. MLX/M5 figures cited in this survey are model- and protocol-specific and must not be extrapolated to Manwe detector latency without reproduction.

The pragmatic architecture: **train canonically on CUDA in BF16, use Apple Silicon (MPS/MLX) for local dev and small fine-tunes, and unify deployment through [ONNX Runtime execution providers](https://onnxruntime.ai/docs/execution-providers/TensorRT-ExecutionProvider.html)** (CoreML EP on Apple; TensorRT/CUDA EP on NVIDIA) + [coremltools 9.0](https://github.com/apple/coremltools/releases) for `.mlmodelc` + MLX for safetensors.

### Comparison table

| Tool / technique | Year | What it is | Why for manwe | Link |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PyTorch MPS + CUDA** | 2022-26 | Same codebase, two backends (MPS beta, CUDA reference) | Most portable train-and-export path; CUDA = production, MPS = dev | [Apple](https://developer.apple.com/metal/pytorch/) |
| **ONNX Runtime + EPs** | 2024-26 | One graph → CoreML/CUDA/TensorRT providers | Covers ONNX+CoreML+TensorRT from one artifact | [docs](https://onnxruntime.ai/docs/execution-providers/TensorRT-ExecutionProvider.html) |
| **TensorRT / TRT-EP** | 2024-26 | Graph-optimizing compiler, FP16/INT8 engines | Fastest NVIDIA path (~5× speedup, ~4× size cut INT8); **NVIDIA-only** | [NVIDIA](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/end-to-end-ai-for-nvidia-based-pcs-cuda-and-tensorrt-execution-providers-in-onnx-runtime/) |
| **Core ML + coremltools 9.0** | 2025 | On-device CPU/GPU/ANE + converter | `.mlmodelc`; verify actual compute-unit placement and semantic parity on target hardware | [releases](https://github.com/apple/coremltools/releases) |
| **Apple MLX** | 2024-26 | Native Apple array/training framework, safetensors | MLX-safetensors target; best Apple-side perf | [repo](https://github.com/ml-explore/mlx) |

| **BF16/FP16 AMP** | 2024-26 | Mixed precision, BF16 needs no loss scaling | Portable default across CUDA+MPS | [HF](https://huggingface.co/docs/transformers/main/perf_train_gpu_one) |

### Recommended picks

- **Two-track training:** CUDA canonical (BF16 AMP + torch.compile + FlashAttention/SDPA + DDP/FSDP2); Apple (MPS/MLX) for dev, small fine-tunes, export validation. Do not train production detectors solely on MPS.
- **BF16 = portable precision.** FP8/FP4 NVIDIA-only, always validated on real hardware.
- **ONNX = export hub** → CoreML EP (Apple), TensorRT EP (pre-built/cached per compute capability) + CUDA EP (NVIDIA).
- **MPS hardening:** pin PyTorch ≥2.4, `PYTORCH_ENABLE_MPS_FALLBACK=1` with logging, eager mode, CI NaN/no-silent-offload assertion.
- **Benchmarking:** extend the bounded microbenchmarks toward a custom,
  MLPerf-inspired protocol—without claiming MLPerf compliance—with warm-up
  discards, 100+ timed iterations, device synchronization, percentiles, and
  pinned versions; a comparable CUDA/TensorRT runner remains future work.
- **Rust:** evaluate Burn + CubeCL for unified train+deploy; keep Candle for inference only.
- **Fusion stack (KF/EKF/UKF/PF/IMM) stays backend-agnostic initially.** Measure
  target-count scaling and end-to-end latency before deciding whether CPU is
  sufficient or acceleration work is justified.

### Caveats & Apple-Silicon gaps

- **A10 beats Apple Silicon on transformer inference** (verified): BERT-base ~23.5 ms CUDA vs ~38 ms M2-Max MLX vs ~179 ms M1 MLX; Apple viable for research/prototyping, full production impractical on consumer devices ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.18921)). *Note: encoder-only study, do not over-extrapolate to large generative models.*
- **⚠️ Caution — "MPS torch.compile falls back to CPU" is oversimplified/partly outdated.** The headline (MPS materially lags CUDA for training) and four of five specifics (no native FP8/FP4, no float64, Gloo-only, no native FlashAttention) are **verified**, but PyTorch ~2.5-2.6 added a native **Metal Inductor codegen path** (buggy/experimental, not a blanket CPU fallback). Also, FP8 on MPS often **errors out** rather than transparently upcasting to BF16. ([source](https://tunguz.github.io/PyTorch_Hardware_2025/))
- M5 gains (up to 4× TTFT, 19-27% token-gen, 120→153 GB/s) are **verified** but are MLX-specific benchmarks on particular models, and bandwidth remains ~22× below H100.
- JACCL/WWDC26 multi-Mac training **closes the memory gap, not the bandwidth gap** — a discrete NVIDIA GPU still wins on raw training throughput.
- Emulated FP8/FP4 on MPS can **silently diverge** from real NVFP4 hardware — validate low-precision runs on the actual NVIDIA GPU.

## Recommended architecture & pipelines for manwe

This section is a **target architecture**, not a statement that every module,
backend or consumer adapter exists. The current five-class candidate taxonomy,
raw exports and local measurement objects do not yet honor crebain's runtime
contracts without the adapters in the compatibility matrix.

### Repo module layout

```
manwe/
├── vision/              # detector zoo: RF-DETR (accuracy) + YOLO26/YOLO11 (tooling)
│   ├── train/           #   config-driven (Hydra); SAHI sliced FT; Mosaic+MixUp+CopyPaste; EMA/cosine/BF16-AMP
│   ├── heads/           #   P2 stride-4 head; optional QueryDet sparse queries; TPH attention heads
│   └── export/          #   target: backend-specific conversion + adapters + gates
├── acoustic/            # SELD ResNet-Conformer (Multi-ACCDOA) + SSLAM/EAT classifier
│   ├── features/        #   log-mel + SALSA-Lite + GCC-PHAT/SRP-PHAT auxiliaries
│   ├── spl/             #   calibrated per-mic dB SPL (computed, not learned)
│   └── fusion_av/       #   AV-FDTI baseline → AV-DTEC-style cross-attention
├── multicam/            # pure linear-algebra geometry (Rust nalgebra / numpy)
│   ├── calib/           #   Zhang/OpenCV → COLMAP/PnP → GeoCalib/AnyCalib tiers
│   ├── triangulate/     #   DLT-SVD + midpoint-ray LSQ + RANSAC + LOSTU covariance
│   └── assoc/           #   epipolar/Sampson gating + OSNet/TransReID
├── fusion/              # Python Stone Soup reference harness (offline)
│   ├── reference/       #   CV 6-state + Mahalanobis gating + GNN/JPDA + M-of-N + deleter
│   ├── metrics/         #   GOSPA/T-GOSPA (primary) + TrackEval HOTA/IDF1
│   └── sim/             #   MultiTargetGroundTruthSimulator: synthetic multi-modal scenarios
├── tracker_rs/          # production real-time Rust tracker (Metal/edge), CV/IMM hand-rolled
├── export/              # AP50 + operating-point + direct-agreement gate; consumer gates planned
└── bench/               # target: comparable custom protocol across supported backends
```

### Default models, datasets, libraries

**Proposed vision evaluation.** Compare a license-reviewed RF-DETR checkpoint with a
YOLO tooling track on the actual aerial data before selecting a default. Evaluate
P2 and SAHI as separate ablations. Candidate data include Anti-UAV, Drone-vs-Bird,
VisDrone/DOTA and ARD-MAV, subject to exact version/access/rights review. Any
open-vocabulary auto-labels remain untrusted annotations until human QA; report
threshold-specific AP separately from canonical COCO metrics.

**Proposed acoustic evaluation.** Keep the implemented SRP-PHAT path as the
classical baseline. Evaluate a SELD/Multi-ACCDOA model only after its dataset and
range-observability assumptions are pinned. DOA, distance and calibrated SPL need
separate uncertainty models and an explicit consumer frame/time adapter. External
audio checkpoints, datasets and CoreML conversions require their own rights,
fidelity and target-hardware validation.

**Proposed multi-camera contract.** Extend the implemented DLT/midpoint baseline
with a versioned calibration schema containing intrinsics, distortion, extrinsics,
units, frame identity and calibration provenance. Add synchronized frame IDs,
correspondence/RANSAC and covariance validation before handing `X_world` to a
tracker. Synthetic geometry is useful for exact fixtures but does not replace a
real synchronized rig evaluation.

**Proposed fusion validation.** Treat Manwe's NumPy tracker as one implementation,
not a golden oracle. Build independently specified fixtures that expose predictions,
innovations, assignments, posteriors and lifecycle decisions, then compare against
a second implementation such as Stone Soup where algorithms overlap. GOSPA and
video metrics answer different questions and should be reported separately. A
production Rust tracker and heterogeneous consumer bridge remain future work.

**Training & export target.** Keep one pinned checkpoint/provenance root, then
produce separately validated ONNX, TensorRT and compiled CoreML artifacts; MLX
requires a future implemented converter rather than a safetensors rename. Training
recipes and calibration-set sizes are experiment variables, not universal
defaults. The current Manwe fidelity helper gates AP50/AP50-small drift,
deployed-threshold precision/recall/FPPI, and direct per-frame/class box/score
agreement against source-pixel ground truth; a full COCO sweep and consumer
end-to-end fixtures are still needed.

**Proposed hardware validation.** Use CUDA for large training when the selected
model and environment benefit from it; use MPS for bounded development only after
operator/numerical checks. Evaluate ONNX Runtime, CoreML and TensorRT as separate
runtime contracts rather than interchangeable execution providers. TensorRT is
NVIDIA-specific, MLX conversion is unimplemented here, and claimed CoreML/ANE
placement must be profiled. First unify benchmark accuracy and timing scopes, then
extend the harness to additional hardware.

### Licensing gate

Prefer **Apache-2.0** for anything shipped (RF-DETR N–L, D-FINE, DEIM, RT-DETRv2/v4). Treat **AGPL-3.0** Ultralytics YOLO and AlbumentationsX as research/benchmark tracks or license commercially. **Confirm RF-DETR-XL/2XL are PML-1.0, not Apache** before shipping the 60-AP flagship.
